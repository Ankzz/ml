**Q6. (Multiplicity)**  
If a matrix has eigenvalue $\lambda = 4$ with algebraic multiplicity 3 but geometric multiplicity 1, the matrix is:  
- A. Diagonalizable  
- B. Defective  
- C. Orthogonal  
- D. Positive definite  

---

**Q7. (PCA & Determinants)**  
The determinant of the covariance matrix in PCA equals:  
- A. The sum of eigenvalues  
- B. The product of eigenvalues (variance volume)  
- C. Always zero  
- D. The trace of the covariance matrix  

---

### ✅ Consolidated Answers
1. A (det = $2\cdot4 - 3\cdot1 = 8 - 3 = 5$)  
2. A (roots of $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0$ → 1, 3)  
3. C ($5 / (5+2+1) = 5/8 = 62.5%$) → correction: actually 62.5%, so answer is **B**  
4. B (Jordan block with eigenvalue 2 and superdiagonal 1)  
5. B (positive definite quadratic minimized at origin)  
6. B (GM < AM → defective)  
7. B (product of eigenvalues = determinant)
