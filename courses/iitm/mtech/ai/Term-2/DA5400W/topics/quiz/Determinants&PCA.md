### 📘 Quiz: PCA & Determinants  

**Q1. (PCA)**  
Which of the following best describes the first principal component in PCA?  
- A. The direction of minimum variance in the data  
- B. The direction orthogonal to all other components  
- C. The direction that maximizes variance in the data  
- D. The direction that minimizes correlation among features  
**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** The first principal component is the axis along which the data has maximum variance.  

---

**Q2. (PCA)**  
If the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix are \([5, 2, 0.5]\), how much variance is explained by the first principal component?  
- A. 50%  
- B. 62.5%  
- C. 71.4%  
- D. 80%  
**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Variance explained = \(5 / (5+2+0.5) = 5/7.5 = 0.667 \approx 71.4\%\).  

---

**Q3. (Determinants)**  
For a 2×2 matrix \(\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}\), the determinant equals:  
- A. \(a+b+c+d\)  
- B. \(ad - bc\)  
- C. \(ab + cd\)  
- D. \(ac - bd\)  
**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The determinant of a 2×2 matrix is \(ad - bc\).  

---

**Q4. (Determinants)**  
Which property of determinants is true?  
- A. \(\det(A+B) = \det(A) + \det(B)\)  
- B. \(\det(AB) = \det(A)\det(B)\)  
- C. \(\det(A^T) = -\det(A)\)  
- D. \(\det(kA) = k \det(A)\) for all \(k\)  
**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Determinants are multiplicative: \(\det(AB) = \det(A)\det(B)\).  

---

**Q5. (PCA + Determinants)**  
Why is the determinant of the covariance matrix important in PCA?  
- A. It equals the sum of eigenvalues  
- B. It equals the product of eigenvalues, representing total variance volume  
- C. It determines the number of principal components  
- D. It is always zero in PCA  
**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The determinant of the covariance matrix is the product of eigenvalues, representing the volume scaling of variance in the transformed space.
